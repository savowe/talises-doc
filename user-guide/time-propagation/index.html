Propagate the wave-function

The heart of TALISES lies in the ability to propagate a wave-function \( \hat{U} |\Psi(r,t_0)\rangle = |\Psi(r,t)\rangle\) in accordance to the Schrödinger equation

$$i\hbar\frac{\partial}{\partial t} |\Psi(r,t)\rangle = \Big( -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \nabla^2 + \hat{V} (\Psi, r, t) \Big) |\Psi(r,t)\rangle .$$
The wave-function includes internal and external degrees of freedom and you can easily implement your own position-, time-dependent or nonlinear potential \( \hat{V} (\Psi, r, t) \).
The configuration of the time-propagation and Hamiltonian is done using an XML-configuration file. Important XML-tags are:

  • <DIM>: number of spatial dimensions
  • <INTERNAL_DIM>: number of internal degrees of freedom
  • <FILENAME>: name of the binary file representing the wave-function generated by gen_psi_0. There need to be as many files as internal degrees of freedom
  • <M>: mass \(m\) of quantum particle as defined in the Schrödinger equation
  • <L>: free scaling parameter for space \(r=Lr'\)
  • <T>: free scaling parameter for time \(t = Tt'\)
  • <CONSTANTS>: here we define constants used in the Hamiltonian's expression
  • <SEQUENCE>: here we declare the Hamiltonian and which numerical procedure to use
<SIMULATION>
  <DIM>1</DIM>
  <INTERNAL_DIM>2</INTERNAL_DIM>
  <FILENAME>0.000_1.bin</FILENAME>
  <FILENAME_2>0.000_2.bin</FILENAME_2>
  <L>1e-6</L>
  <T>1e-6</T>
  <m>1.44466899e-25</m>
  <CONSTANTS>
    <f_R>0.0025</f_R>
  </CONSTANTS>
  <SEQUENCE>
    <interact Nk="500" dt="0.02" output_freq="last" pn_freq="each"
      V_11_real="0" V_11_imag="0" 
      V_12_real="2*pi*f_R/2*cos(Delta*t)" V_12_imag="2*pi*f_R/2*sin(Delta*t)"
      V_22_real="Delta" V_22_imag="0"
    >100</interact>
    <freeprop Nk="10000" dt="0.2" output_freq="each" pn_freq="each"
      V_11_real="0" V_11_imag="0"   V_22_real="Delta" V_22_imag="0"
    >6000</freeprop>
  </SEQUENCE>
</SIMULATION>

Scaling parameter T and L

Time and length scales of quantum mechanical system vary vastly across different applications. In order to avoid counting down 12 or more decimals when setting up the wave-function or time-propagation sequence, your have the possibility to apply scale factors \(L\) and \(T\) to position and time variables.
After substitution \(r=Lr'\) and \(t=Tt'\) into the Schrödinger equation we obtain the dimensionless Schrödinger equation $$ i\frac{\partial}{\partial t'}\Psi = \Big( -\frac{\hbar T}{2mL^2} \nabla '^{\,2} + \frac{T}{\hbar} V(Lr',Tt') \Big) \Psi $$
